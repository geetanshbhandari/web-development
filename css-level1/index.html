Who Is Iron Man?

Iron Man is a superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The character was co-created by writer and editor Stan Lee, developed by scripter Larry Lieber, and designed by artists Don Heck and Jack Kirby. His first appearance was in Tales of Suspense #39 (March 1963), and he received his own title with Iron Man #1 (May 1968).

Publication History

Premiere

Iron Man's premiere in Tales of Suspense #39 (1963) was a collaboration among editor Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, and story-artist Don Heck. Stan Lee wanted to create an arms dealer as a superhero and make readers love him anyway.

Solo Series

After issue #99 (March 1968), Tales of Suspense was renamed Captain America. Iron Man debuted solo with Iron Man #1 (May 1968), also known as The Invincible Iron Man.

Fictional Biography

Anthony Edward Stark — Tony Stark — is the son of Howard Stark, a wealthy industrialist who leads Stark Industries. A boy genius, he entered MIT at age 15 to study engineering and physics. After his parents were killed in a car accident, he inherited the company and became one of the most powerful people on the planet.
